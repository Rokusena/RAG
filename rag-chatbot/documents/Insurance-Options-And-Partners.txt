Insurance Options and Partners
================================

1. Introduction
Vehicle insurance is a legal requirement and a critical protection for any car owner. AutoGroup Motors partners with leading Lithuanian insurance providers to offer comprehensive coverage options at competitive rates. This document explains the types of vehicle insurance available, our partner offerings, and how we help customers secure the best policy for their needs.

2. Mandatory Insurance: TPVCA (Third-Party Vehicle Civil Liability Insurance)
All vehicles registered in Lithuania must have valid TPVCA insurance, commonly known as "civilinė" or "draudimas." This is the minimum legal requirement and covers damage you cause to other people, vehicles, or property in the event of an accident. It does not cover damage to your own vehicle.

TPVCA premiums are calculated based on the vehicle owner's age, driving experience, claims history, vehicle type, engine size, and region of registration. Annual premiums typically range from EUR 60 to EUR 400 for standard passenger vehicles. AutoGroup Motors ensures every vehicle sold has valid TPVCA coverage before delivery, and we assist with policy transfer or new policy setup as part of the handover process.

3. Comprehensive Insurance: KASKO
KASKO insurance provides comprehensive coverage for your own vehicle, including damage from accidents (regardless of fault), theft, vandalism, natural disasters, fire, glass breakage, and animal collisions. KASKO is not legally required but is highly recommended, especially for financed or leased vehicles where lenders typically require it.

KASKO premiums vary based on the vehicle's value, make, model, age, security features, the driver's profile, and the deductible amount chosen. Annual premiums typically range from 2% to 6% of the vehicle's insured value. For a vehicle valued at EUR 15,000, expect annual KASKO premiums between EUR 300 and EUR 900.

Coverage options within KASKO policies include:
- Full comprehensive coverage (all risks)
- Partial coverage (theft and total loss only)
- Named perils coverage (specific risks selected by the policyholder)

5. Gap Insurance
For customers financing their vehicle purchase, we recommend considering gap insurance. Gap insurance covers the difference between the outstanding loan balance and the vehicle's market value at the time of a total loss event. This is particularly important for new and recently imported vehicles, which may depreciate faster than the loan is paid down. Gap insurance premiums start from EUR 99 per year.

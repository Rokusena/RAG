Vehicle Comparison and Buyer's Guide
======================================

1. Introduction
Choosing the right vehicle can be overwhelming, especially with the wide range of makes, models, and configurations available in the pre-owned market. This guide is designed to help AutoGroup Motors customers understand the key differences between popular vehicle categories and make an informed purchasing decision based on their lifestyle, budget, and driving needs.

2. Sedans
Best for: Daily commuting, fuel efficiency, city and highway driving
Popular models in our inventory: Volkswagen Passat, Toyota Camry, BMW 3 Series, Audi A4, Mazda 6

Sedans offer a balanced combination of comfort, fuel efficiency, and handling. They are ideal for individuals and small families who primarily drive on paved roads and value low running costs. Modern sedans come equipped with advanced safety features, infotainment systems, and comfortable interiors. In the Lithuanian market, the Volkswagen Passat and BMW 3 Series are among the most popular choices due to their reliability, brand recognition, and availability of parts and service.

Fuel economy: Petrol sedans typically achieve 6 to 8 liters per 100 km, while diesel variants can achieve 4.5 to 6 liters per 100 km. Hybrid options such as the Toyota Camry Hybrid can achieve under 5 liters per 100 km in mixed driving.

3. SUVs and Crossovers
Best for: Families, outdoor activities, unpaved roads, winter driving conditions
Popular models in our inventory: Toyota RAV4, Volkswagen Tiguan, BMW X3, Hyundai Tucson, Kia Sportage

SUVs and crossovers have become the most popular vehicle category in Lithuania and across Europe. They offer higher ground clearance, more cargo space, and often come with all-wheel drive systems that provide better traction in winter conditions — a significant advantage in the Lithuanian climate. However, they generally consume more fuel and have higher insurance premiums compared to sedans of similar value.

Fuel economy: Petrol SUVs typically consume 7.5 to 10 liters per 100 km, while diesel SUVs achieve 6 to 8 liters per 100 km. Hybrid and plug-in hybrid SUVs like the Toyota RAV4 Hybrid can achieve under 5 liters per 100 km in mixed driving.

4. Hatchbacks and Compact Cars
Best for: City driving, tight parking, young drivers, budget-conscious buyers
Popular models in our inventory: Volkswagen Golf, Honda Civic, Mazda 3, Ford Focus, Peugeot 308

Hatchbacks and compact cars offer excellent maneuverability in urban environments, easy parking, and generally lower purchase and running costs. Despite their smaller size, modern hatchbacks provide surprisingly spacious interiors and well-equipped feature lists. Performance-oriented variants like the Honda Civic Type R or Volkswagen Golf GTI offer sporty driving dynamics while retaining everyday practicality.

Fuel economy: Petrol hatchbacks typically achieve 5.5 to 7.5 liters per 100 km, with diesel variants achieving 4 to 5.5 liters per 100 km.

5. Electric and Hybrid Vehicles
Best for: Environmentally conscious buyers, low running costs, city driving, tech enthusiasts
Models we source upon request: Tesla Model 3, Nissan Leaf, Hyundai Ioniq 5, BMW iX3, Volkswagen ID.4

Electric vehicles (EVs) have zero tailpipe emissions and significantly lower running costs compared to petrol or diesel vehicles. Charging at home overnight using a standard Lithuanian electricity tariff costs approximately EUR 3 to EUR 5 for a full charge providing 250 to 400 km of range. Public charging infrastructure in Lithuania is expanding, with fast chargers available on major highways and in city centers.

Key considerations for EV buyers in Lithuania: Battery health and remaining warranty are critical factors when purchasing a used EV. Cold weather reduces battery range by 15 to 30 percent during Lithuanian winters. Plug-in hybrids (PHEVs) offer a compromise, providing 40 to 80 km of electric-only range combined with a petrol or diesel engine for longer trips.

6. Diesel vs. Petrol vs. Hybrid: What to Choose
Diesel advantages: Better fuel economy, higher torque for towing and highway cruising, longer engine lifespan. Diesel disadvantages: Higher purchase price, more expensive maintenance (DPF filter, AdBlue), potential future restrictions in city centers, less suitable for short city trips.

Petrol advantages: Lower purchase price, smoother and quieter engine operation, more suitable for short trips and city driving, simpler maintenance. Petrol disadvantages: Higher fuel consumption compared to diesel, lower torque output.

Hybrid advantages: Excellent fuel economy in city driving, lower emissions, reduced fuel costs, regenerative braking. Hybrid disadvantages: Higher initial purchase price, battery replacement costs (though modern batteries last 8 to 15 years), slightly more complex maintenance.

7. Budget Guidelines
We recommend the following budget allocation for vehicle ownership:
- Purchase price: Should not exceed 3 to 6 months of your net household income
- Annual running costs (fuel, insurance, maintenance, taxes): Budget EUR 1,500 to EUR 3,000 for a typical pre-owned vehicle
- Emergency repair fund: Set aside EUR 500 to EUR 1,000 for unexpected repairs

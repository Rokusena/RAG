Employee Handbook and Code of Conduct
=======================================

1. Welcome
Welcome to AutoGroup Motors. This handbook outlines the expectations, policies, and guidelines that all employees must follow. Our success depends on maintaining a professional, ethical, and customer-focused work environment. Every team member plays a critical role in upholding our reputation and delivering exceptional service.

2. Working Hours and Attendance
Standard working hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 to 18:00, with a one-hour lunch break. Saturday shifts run from 9:00 to 15:00 and are rotated among sales and service staff on a monthly schedule. All employees are expected to arrive on time and notify their supervisor at least one hour before their shift if they will be late or absent. Repeated tardiness or unexcused absences may result in disciplinary action.

3. Dress Code
All customer-facing employees must wear the company uniform, which consists of a branded polo shirt, dark trousers or skirt, and closed-toe shoes. Service and workshop staff must wear the provided safety gear, including steel-toe boots, gloves, and protective eyewear when performing mechanical work. Uniforms must be clean and presentable at all times.

4. Customer Interaction Standards
Our customers are the foundation of our business. Every interaction should be professional, courteous, and helpful. Employees must greet every customer within 60 seconds of their arrival at the dealership. Sales consultants should actively listen to customer needs, provide honest and accurate information about vehicles, and never pressure customers into a purchase. Misleading statements about vehicle condition, history, or pricing are strictly prohibited and are grounds for immediate termination.

5. Sales Ethics and Transparency
AutoGroup Motors is committed to full transparency in all sales transactions. Every vehicle listing must accurately represent the vehicle's condition, mileage, history, and any known defects. Odometer tampering, concealing accident history, or misrepresenting vehicle specifications are criminal offenses under Lithuanian law and will result in immediate dismissal and legal action. All sales agreements must be reviewed with the customer, and all fees, taxes, and charges must be clearly explained before signing.

6. Data Protection and Privacy
In compliance with the European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), all employees must handle customer personal data with the utmost care. Customer information collected during sales, financing, or service interactions must be stored securely in our CRM system and may only be accessed by authorized personnel for legitimate business purposes. Sharing customer data with third parties without explicit consent is prohibited. Employees must complete the mandatory annual GDPR training module.

7. Workplace Safety
AutoGroup Motors is committed to providing a safe working environment. Employees must report any safety hazards, injuries, or near-misses to their supervisor immediately. The workshop area has specific safety protocols, including mandatory use of personal protective equipment, proper handling of chemicals and fluids, and adherence to fire safety procedures. Emergency exits and fire extinguisher locations are marked throughout the facility. Fire drills are conducted quarterly.

8. Leave and Benefits
Full-time employees are entitled to 20 working days of annual leave, accrued monthly. Sick leave is granted in accordance with Lithuanian labor law, with the first two days paid by the employer and subsequent days covered by Sodra (the State Social Insurance Fund). Employees are also entitled to public holidays as defined by Lithuanian law. Additional benefits include a company phone, a vehicle purchase discount of 10%, and access to the employee wellness program.

9. Disciplinary Procedure
Violations of company policy will be addressed through a progressive disciplinary process: verbal warning, written warning, final written warning, and termination. Serious offenses such as fraud, theft, harassment, discrimination, or gross negligence may result in immediate termination without prior warnings. All disciplinary actions are documented and stored in the employee's personnel file.

10. Contact
For HR-related questions, contact hr@autogroupmotors.lt or visit the HR office during business hours.

Trade-In and Vehicle Appraisal Guide
======================================

1. Overview
AutoGroup Motors accepts trade-ins on all vehicle purchases. Trading in your current vehicle is one of the easiest ways to reduce the cost of your next car while avoiding the hassle of selling privately. Our appraisal process is transparent, fast, and based on fair market data.

2. How the Trade-In Process Works
Step 1: Bring your vehicle to our dealership or submit an online appraisal request through our website. For online requests, you will need to provide the vehicle make, model, year, mileage, VIN, and recent photographs showing the exterior, interior, engine bay, and any damage.

Step 2: Our appraisal team conducts an in-person inspection, which typically takes 20 to 30 minutes. The inspection covers mechanical condition, body and paint condition, interior wear, tire condition, service history, and any aftermarket modifications. We also run a history check to verify ownership records, accident history, and outstanding finance.

Step 3: Based on the inspection and current market data from platforms such as Autoplius, Mobile.de, and AutoScout24, we provide a written trade-in offer. This offer is valid for 7 calendar days or 500 kilometers driven, whichever comes first.

Step 4: If you accept the trade-in offer, the value is applied directly to the purchase price of your new vehicle. This reduces the amount you need to finance or pay in cash. All paperwork for the transfer of ownership is handled by our team at no extra cost.

3. Factors That Affect Trade-In Value
The following factors are considered when determining your trade-in value:
- Make, model, and year: Popular brands and models (Volkswagen, Toyota, BMW, Audi) tend to hold their value better in the Lithuanian market.
- Mileage: Lower mileage vehicles generally receive higher valuations. Vehicles with over 200,000 km will see a more significant depreciation adjustment.
- Condition: Vehicles in excellent mechanical and cosmetic condition command higher trade-in values. Major mechanical issues, significant body damage, or heavy interior wear will reduce the offer.
- Service history: A complete and documented service history, especially from authorized service centers, adds value. Missing service records create uncertainty about the vehicle's maintenance and lower the valuation.
- Market demand: Trade-in values are influenced by current market demand. SUVs and crossovers currently command premium prices in Lithuania, while sedans and older diesel vehicles have seen declining demand due to environmental concerns and potential future restrictions in city centers.
- Equipment and features: Desirable options such as automatic transmission, navigation, leather interior, parking sensors, and advanced safety features can increase the trade-in value.

4. Vehicles We Accept for Trade-In
We accept most passenger vehicles, SUVs, and light commercial vehicles regardless of make or origin. However, we reserve the right to decline a trade-in offer for vehicles that are not roadworthy, have outstanding legal encumbrances (such as unpaid fines or active leasing contracts not yet settled), or are older than 20 years with significant mechanical issues.

5. Trade-In vs. Private Sale
While private sales may yield a higher price in some cases, trade-ins offer significant advantages: no advertising costs, no need to meet with multiple buyers, no risk of fraud, instant value applied to your purchase, and all paperwork handled for you. For customers who need to sell quickly or want a hassle-free experience, the trade-in option is the most practical choice.

6. Enhancing Your Trade-In Value
Before bringing your vehicle for appraisal, we recommend:
- Thoroughly cleaning the interior and exterior
- Gathering all service records, receipts, and the vehicle manual
- Fixing minor cosmetic issues (small dents, scratches, burned-out bulbs)
- Ensuring all keys, spare tire, and tools are present
- Addressing any warning lights on the dashboard if possible

These small steps can noticeably improve the perceived condition of your vehicle and result in a better offer.
